About The Project

Happy Hills Animal Foundation is one of only two state-licensed shelter facilities in Randolph County, North Carolina. The foundation has been providing shelter for homeless animals for over 40 years.

Recently, they've been facing challenges including increased demand and damage from a fire. The shelter needs more dog houses and supplies to continue their vital work.

Our initiative aims to rally local lumber and construction companies to donate materials and expertise, while also raising funds for additional supplies. With your help, we can make a significant difference in the lives of these animals.

The foundation receives no government funding and is funded primarily from private donors, sponsors, and volunteers like you!

Volunteer Opportunities

How You Can Help

We need volunteers with various skills to help make this project a success:

  • Carpenters and builders to construct dog houses
  • Drivers to transport materials and completed houses
  • Coordinators to organize volunteers and donations
  • Social media managers to spread the word
  • Anyone willing to lend a helping hand!

No experience? No problem! We'll provide guidance for those who want to help but don't have specific skills.
